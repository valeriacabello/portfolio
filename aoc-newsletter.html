Newsletter

The Arizona Outdoors Club was struggling to keep registered and prospective members informed about club activities and news. To resolve this, I developed a brand strategy to execute into a newsletter that attracts and keeps the audience engaged.



Process

As the new graphic designer and marketing director for the club, I looked through marketing projects and design work that existed before I joined to see what I had to work with. When I looked at the newsletter, I saw a huge opportunity. All I saw on it were blocks of text and not much consideration for hierarchy and color. The only form of branding I could see was at the top on the header image.

What was given to me:

I tackled this as a challenge of creating a new brand that would be consistent not just on the newsletter but also on merch, flyers, and beyond. After that, I went back to the newsletter and approached it with a strong use of imagery in mind.

Outdoor excursions are the main event at the outdoors club so why not make them the center of the newsletter? I knew that this was something club members were most interested in being informed about because I talked to them during my time in the club.

I had this idea of using one big image inspiring the outdoors lifestyle to attract readers. The image would be one that was taken by a club member and I'd give them the attribution to show a sense of community.

Over this image, I would list the outdoor trips that were available so that the reader could see right away what trips they could go on. I also wanted to make sure that the readers could see who the email is from at the top.

In the body section of the newsletter, I wanted to have important announcements display first in their own section, followed by a section for upcoming trips.

Each trip would use an image to make the email look less wordy and keep the readers engaged. The image would be followed by details about the trip.

Below the trips section, I'd include any other relevant club events and instructions on how to become a member.

For the footer, I wanted to have a call to action and show the club's sponsors.

I wanted to make sure to include helpful links, social media profiles, and a reply button. The sponsors would be important to include as a thank you and to let the reader know about discounts they were offered.

I took all of those ideas to create the newsletter on MailChimp using the brand that I designed. At the end of my first year as marketing director and graphic designer, I conducted a survey to learn about what club members enjoyed seeing on the newsletter and what they wish they could've seen. I then implemented the findings into my designs the following year.
